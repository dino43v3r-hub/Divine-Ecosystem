## Purpose

This document defines how Divine Ecosystem is stewarded, how delegated project authority is exercised, and how governance decisions remain accountable to Holy Scripture and the Divine Ecosystem Constitution.

Governance exists to preserve theological faithfulness, constitutional consistency, transparent decision-making, responsible review, canonical integrity, and long-term project continuity.

This document establishes roles, delegated authorities, approval responsibilities, review requirements, decision governance, release governance, and stewardship continuity. It governs who may authorize project actions and the limits under which that authority may be exercised.

Governance does not create theological truth, constitutional authority, architectural implementation, development workflow, or project priorities. Those responsibilities remain with the canonical documents to which they belong.

---

# Governance Principles

## Scripture Remains Supreme

Holy Scripture is the supreme authority governing every theological principle, canonical document, governance decision, architectural decision, and future application within Divine Ecosystem.

No governance role, decision, approval, consensus, process, or institutional structure may possess authority equal to or greater than Holy Scripture.

## The Constitution Remains Controlling

The Divine Ecosystem Constitution is the enduring governing charter of the project and remains continually subject to Holy Scripture.

Every governance decision shall remain consistent with the Constitution. Where a governance decision conflicts with the Constitution, the Constitution shall prevail and the conflicting decision shall have no governing force.

## Stewardship Is Delegated and Limited

Governance authority is a responsibility entrusted for the faithful stewardship of Divine Ecosystem. It is not spiritual, ecclesial, or theological authority.

Every governance role may exercise only the authority explicitly delegated to that role. No role may enlarge its own authority through precedent, convenience, urgency, or repeated practice.

## Roles Remain Conceptually Distinct

Divine Ecosystem uses a scale-neutral governance model. Role separation is conceptual rather than necessarily organizational.

During the early stages of Divine Ecosystem, one individual may fulfill multiple governance roles. However, the responsibilities, delegated authorities, review requirements, and accountability associated with each role remain separate and should be exercised as though they were distinct offices whenever practical.

When one person acts in multiple roles, the project record should identify the capacity in which each decision, review, approval, or release action was performed. As additional qualified contributors join the project, roles may be assigned to different people without changing the responsibilities or limits established by this document.

## Decisions Shall Be Transparent

Significant decisions shall preserve the reasoning, alternatives, impact, and authority under which they were made.

Approval shall never substitute for explanation. Governance decisions should remain understandable and reviewable by future contributors.

## Approval Does Not Establish Infallibility

Canonical approval establishes the current governing state of a project document. It does not make a human decision, interpretation, document, reviewer, or governance process infallible.

Every subordinate decision remains open to examination and correction under Holy Scripture and the Constitution.

## Review Shall Be Proportionate

The depth and independence of review shall reflect the significance of the proposed change. Minor editorial work requires less review than canonical, constitutional, or architectural work.

The review model may expand as the project matures, but required review shall not be reduced merely for speed or convenience.

## Canonical Documentation Preserves Continuity

Canonical documents and recorded decisions govern the project. Conversations, informal understandings, and undocumented practices do not replace the canonical record.

Material governance decisions shall be preserved in the appropriate canonical document or decision record.

## Small, Reviewable Progress Is Preferred

Governance should favor focused, understandable, and verifiable changes. Large changes should be divided into smaller reviewable phases whenever doing so preserves the integrity of the work.

# Governing Authority

Governance operates within the following order of authority:

```text
Holy Scripture
        ↓
Divine Ecosystem Constitution
        ↓
Foundational Principles
        ↓
Governance
        ↓
Subordinate governance decisions and delegated actions
```

Holy Scripture remains the supreme authority. The Constitution derives its authority from and remains subject to Holy Scripture. The Foundational Principles describe the enduring philosophy and design principles of the project in submission to both Holy Scripture and the Constitution.

Governance receives delegated authority to steward the project within these boundaries. Governance does not originate independent authority and may not transfer authority that it does not possess.

Authority order is not development order. The sequence in which documents are researched, drafted, reviewed, or adopted does not alter their place within the canonical hierarchy.

# Scope and Boundaries

## Governance Responsibilities

The following responsibilities belong in Governance:

* project stewardship,
* delegated decision authority,
* canonical approval authority,
* review requirements,
* contributor responsibilities and approval boundaries,
* constitutional amendment intake and review administration,
* release approval,
* decision accountability,
* conflict escalation,
* correction of unauthorized or noncompliant changes,
* and long-term stewardship continuity.

## Responsibilities Assigned Elsewhere

Governance shall preserve the responsibility of every other canonical document.

### Constitution

The Constitution establishes enduring theological and constitutional principles. Governance may administer constitutional review but may not create constitutional amendment principles or redefine constitutional authority.

### Architecture

Architecture defines system structure, reasoning pipelines, engines, interfaces, data flow, and implementation. Governance may require architectural review and approve whether a change may proceed, but it may not make architectural decisions in place of Architecture.

### Project Workflow

Project Workflow defines the process used to discover, draft, review, revise, verify, and maintain canonical documents. Governance defines who has authority and responsibility at the applicable stages; it does not duplicate or silently replace the workflow.

### Contribution Standards

Contribution Standards define the substantive standards by which contributions are evaluated. Governance assigns review and approval responsibility but does not replace those standards.

### Decision Log

The Decision Log preserves the reasoning behind significant decisions. Governance determines when a decision record is required but does not duplicate the log's required format.

### Roadmap

The future Roadmap will define planned project direction. Governance may approve planning decisions but does not establish roadmap content within this document.

### Backlog

The Backlog records unfinished work, proposed work, and matters requiring further discovery. Governance may classify or defer work to the Backlog but does not treat backlog items as adopted decisions.

# Governance Roles

Governance roles identify distinct responsibilities within the project. A role may be held by one person, several qualified people, or a future governance body when that delegation is explicitly recorded.

## Project Steward

The Project Steward is responsible for the faithful, orderly, and continuous stewardship of Divine Ecosystem. The Project Steward coordinates governance decisions, protects canonical integrity, ensures required review occurs, and preserves accountability to Holy Scripture and the Constitution.

## Contributor

A Contributor proposes or prepares changes to project documents, research, architecture, implementation, or other repository material. Human and AI-assisted contributions are subject to the same canonical boundaries and contribution standards.

## Reviewer

A Reviewer evaluates a proposed change for correctness, clarity, scope, internal consistency, and compliance with the documents applicable to that change.

## Independent Reviewer

An Independent Reviewer evaluates a canonical change from a position meaningfully separate from its authorship. Independent review exists to identify assumptions, conflicts, omissions, and unintended effects that may not be visible to the contributor.

## Constitutional Reviewer

A Constitutional Reviewer evaluates proposed constitutional changes for fidelity to Holy Scripture, constitutional coherence, theological integrity, and compliance with the constitutional review process.

Constitutional review does not itself authorize an amendment.

## Architectural Reviewer

An Architectural Reviewer evaluates proposed architectural changes for constitutional consistency, architectural coherence, responsibility boundaries, maintainability, and effects upon the wider ecosystem.

Architectural review shall not begin before Architecture has been canonically established.

## Canonical Approver

The Canonical Approver determines whether a reviewed and validated document or change may enter the current canonical project record.

Initially, the Project Steward serves as the Canonical Approver. This authority may be delegated in the future through an explicit governance decision.

## Release Approver

The Release Approver determines whether a reviewed and validated project state is authorized for release.

Initially, the Project Steward serves as the Release Approver. This authority may be delegated in the future through an explicit governance decision.

# Delegated Authorities

Delegated authority shall be exercised only for its stated purpose and within its stated limitations. When uncertainty exists, the matter shall be escalated rather than resolved through assumed authority.

## Project Steward

### Responsibilities

* preserve constitutional and canonical consistency,
* coordinate governance activity,
* ensure work is classified in the correct document,
* ensure required review and validation occur,
* protect repository and decision continuity,
* identify conflicts or unauthorized changes,
* and maintain clear delegation records.

### Authority

The Project Steward may:

* authorize work to proceed through approved governance and workflow stages,
* assign or recognize qualified reviewers,
* defer unfinished or insufficiently supported work,
* reject work that violates governing documents,
* require additional review,
* act as the initial Canonical Approver,
* act as the initial Release Approver,
* and delegate canonical or release approval authority through a recorded governance decision.

### Limitations

The Project Steward may not:

* supersede Holy Scripture or the Constitution,
* exercise spiritual or ecclesial authority,
* approve a change that has not received its required review,
* convert a backlog item or conversation into a canonical decision without the required process,
* create constitutional amendment principles,
* make architectural decisions before Architecture is established,
* or delegate authority beyond that granted by Governance.

### Escalation Requirements

The Project Steward shall escalate a matter when it:

* may require constitutional amendment,
* presents a material theological conflict,
* falls outside existing delegated authority,
* cannot satisfy its required review level,
* presents an unresolved conflict of interest,
* or would redefine the responsibility of another canonical document.

## Contributor

### Responsibilities

* understand the governing documents applicable to the contribution,
* classify the proposed work correctly,
* disclose material assumptions and uncertainty,
* preserve the approved scope,
* respond transparently to review,
* and avoid unauthorized changes.

### Authority

A Contributor may:

* propose changes,
* prepare drafts within an approved scope,
* explain reasoning and alternatives,
* respond to review findings,
* and recommend that work be approved, revised, deferred, or rejected.

### Limitations

A Contributor may not:

* grant canonical status to their own work,
* bypass required review or validation,
* treat authorship as approval authority,
* introduce out-of-scope changes without review,
* or present AI-generated material as possessing independent authority.

### Escalation Requirements

A Contributor shall escalate questions involving constitutional conflict, uncertain classification, expanded scope, unresolved review findings, or authority not explicitly granted for the work.

## Reviewer

### Responsibilities

* evaluate the assigned scope diligently,
* identify conflicts, omissions, and unintended effects,
* distinguish required corrections from optional recommendations,
* disclose material conflicts of interest,
* and document the review result clearly.

### Authority

A Reviewer may:

* request clarification,
* identify required corrections,
* recommend approval, revision, deferral, rejection, or additional review,
* and withhold a favorable recommendation when applicable standards are not satisfied.

### Limitations

A Reviewer may not:

* rewrite work without authorization,
* expand the approved scope through review comments,
* grant canonical or release approval unless separately delegated that role,
* or substitute personal preference for canonical requirements.

### Escalation Requirements

A Reviewer shall escalate constitutional questions, architectural questions requiring specialized review, material theological concerns, conflicts of interest, and unresolved disagreements that affect approval.

## Independent Reviewer

### Responsibilities

The Independent Reviewer carries the responsibilities of a Reviewer and additionally evaluates whether the proposed canonical change has been examined independently of its authorship.

### Authority

The Independent Reviewer may exercise the review authority defined above and may require unresolved material concerns to be presented to the Canonical Approver.

### Limitations

Independent review remains advisory unless approval authority has been separately and explicitly delegated. Independence does not create constitutional, canonical, or release authority.

### Escalation Requirements

The Independent Reviewer shall escalate any material concern that remains unresolved when canonical approval is requested.

## Constitutional Reviewer

### Responsibilities

* evaluate fidelity to Holy Scripture,
* evaluate constitutional coherence and necessity,
* distinguish constitutional principle from implementation or governance policy,
* examine material theological implications,
* and ensure the reasoning and alternatives are documented.

### Authority

A Constitutional Reviewer may recommend that a proposal proceed, be revised, be deferred, be rejected, or be returned for further theological study.

### Limitations

A Constitutional Reviewer may not create amendment rules, declare an amendment adopted, supersede Scripture, or treat review conclusions as infallible.

### Escalation Requirements

Unresolved questions of scriptural fidelity, constitutional authority, theological meaning, or amendment legitimacy shall be escalated and shall prevent ordinary canonical approval.

## Architectural Reviewer

### Responsibilities

* evaluate architectural consistency and scope,
* protect separation between enduring principles and implementation,
* examine ecosystem-wide effects,
* and identify unnecessary complexity or duplication.

### Authority

Once Architecture is canonically established, an Architectural Reviewer may recommend approval, revision, deferral, rejection, or additional architectural analysis.

### Limitations

An Architectural Reviewer may not redefine constitutional principles, governance policy, or application purpose through architectural review.

### Escalation Requirements

Constitutional conflicts, governance conflicts, cross-application effects, and unresolved material architectural risks shall be escalated before approval.

## Canonical Approver

### Responsibilities

* confirm that the required review and validation are complete,
* confirm that the change belongs in the identified canonical document,
* confirm that material concerns have been resolved or transparently dispositioned,
* and preserve a clear approval record.

### Authority

The Canonical Approver may approve, reject, defer, or return a proposed canonical change for further work.

### Limitations

The Canonical Approver may not waive constitutional authority, required review, or required validation. Canonical approval may not be used to make out-of-scope work authoritative.

### Escalation Requirements

The Canonical Approver shall escalate unresolved constitutional questions, material theological conflicts, unclear authority, and changes that require a form of review that has not been completed.

## Release Approver

### Responsibilities

* confirm that the proposed release contains the approved project state,
* confirm that required validation and repository verification are complete,
* confirm that licensing and intellectual-property requirements have been considered,
* and preserve a release approval record.

### Authority

The Release Approver may authorize, defer, or reject a release.

### Limitations

Release approval does not grant canonical status, cure an unauthorized change, or override unresolved constitutional, legal, security, or validation concerns.

### Escalation Requirements

The Release Approver shall escalate unresolved legal or intellectual-property questions, discrepancies between the approved and proposed release state, missing validation, or conflicts with canonical documents.

# Review Levels

The minimum review level shall be determined by the nature of the change.

## Minor Editorial Changes

Minor editorial changes require one Reviewer.

A minor editorial change corrects spelling, grammar, formatting, broken references, or unambiguous naming without changing meaning, authority, responsibility, policy, or scope.

If a proposed editorial change could alter meaning, it shall be reviewed at the higher applicable level.

## Canonical Document Changes

Changes to the meaning, responsibility, policy, or scope of a canonical document require one Independent Reviewer before canonical approval.

## Constitutional Changes

Constitutional changes require Constitutional Review.

Constitutional Review shall evaluate scriptural fidelity, theological meaning, constitutional necessity, authority, coherence, and long-term consequences. The constitutional amendment principles and adoption requirements must be established by the Constitution and may not be invented by Governance.

## Architectural Changes

Architectural changes require Architectural Review after Architecture has been canonically established.

Architecture shall not begin, and architectural approval shall not be exercised, merely because this review category is defined.

## Expanded Review

The Project Steward or applicable approver may require additional reviewers when a change has broad effects, unusual risk, unresolved disagreement, specialized subject matter, or significant consequences for the project.

Expanded review supplements the minimum requirements and does not reduce or replace them.

# Decision Authority and Classification

Every proposed decision shall be classified before approval.

## Editorial Decisions

Editorial decisions correct presentation without changing meaning, responsibility, authority, or policy. They require the minor editorial review level.

## Canonical Document Decisions

Canonical document decisions change the meaning, scope, responsibility, or policy of a canonical document. They require independent review and Canonical Approver authorization.

## Governance Decisions

Governance decisions assign or exercise delegated authority, establish stewardship policy, approve governance delegations, or resolve matters within the scope of this document.

Material governance decisions shall be recorded in `DECISION_LOG.md`.

## Constitutional Questions

A question that may establish, revise, or remove an enduring theological or constitutional principle shall be classified as a constitutional question. It shall not be resolved through ordinary governance authority.

## Architectural Decisions

A decision concerning system structure, reasoning pipelines, engines, interfaces, data flow, or implementation belongs in Architecture and shall not be resolved in Governance.

## Release Decisions

A release decision authorizes a verified project state for release. It does not independently approve the canonical or technical changes contained in that state.

When classification is uncertain, the decision shall be deferred until the appropriate document and review authority are identified.

# Canonical Document Governance

## Proposed Material

Proposed material has no canonical authority. It may be researched, discussed, or drafted only within an approved scope.

## Draft Status

A Draft is a reviewable document or change that has not received canonical approval. Draft status shall be clearly identified and shall not be represented as governing the project.

## Review Readiness

A draft is ready for review when its purpose, scope, dependencies, material decisions, and known uncertainties are sufficiently clear for meaningful evaluation.

## Validation

Before canonical approval, the proposed change shall be checked for consistency with higher authority, document boundaries, approved decisions, internal references, repository state, and the applicable review findings.

## Canonical Approval

A document or change becomes Canonical only after required review, approved revision, validation, and explicit authorization by the Canonical Approver.

Canonical status represents the current approved project record. It remains subordinate to Holy Scripture and the Constitution and remains open to responsible future correction.

## Working Material

Working material may support research or development but is not canonical. Its status and relationship to canonical documents should be clear.

## Archived Material

Archived material is retained for historical context but no longer governs current project work. Archiving shall preserve enough context to identify what superseded the material and why.

## Reopening Canonical Review

A canonical document may be reopened when new evidence, a discovered conflict, an unresolved constitutional question, or a material project need warrants review.

Reopening review does not suspend the current canonical document unless an authorized governance decision explicitly determines that continued reliance would cause material harm or constitutional conflict.

## Emergency Corrections

An urgent correction may be expedited only when delay would preserve a clear legal, security, repository-integrity, or constitutional defect.

Expedited handling shall preserve the highest applicable authority, document the reason for urgency, limit the change to the defect, and complete any required retrospective review promptly.

Urgency shall not be used to bypass constitutional review or to introduce unrelated changes.

# Constitutional Review and Amendment Administration

Governance administers constitutional amendment intake, review, documentation, and verification. Governance does not create constitutional amendment principles, adoption thresholds, or independent amendment authority.

## Intake

A proposed constitutional change shall identify:

* the constitutional question,
* the existing text affected,
* the proposed enduring principle or correction,
* the reason ordinary governance or architectural action is insufficient,
* known theological implications,
* and material alternatives or uncertainties.

## Review

The proposal shall receive Constitutional Review. Review shall evaluate fidelity to Holy Scripture, constitutional necessity, theological coherence, document classification, and long-term effects.

Governance may coordinate review but may not predetermine its theological conclusion.

## Documentation

The proposal, reasoning, material evidence, alternatives, review findings, unresolved concerns, and disposition shall be preserved in the appropriate canonical record and `DECISION_LOG.md`.

## Verification

If a constitutional change is adopted under principles established by the Constitution, Governance shall verify that the approved text, decision record, document metadata, cross-references, and repository state accurately reflect that adoption.

Until the Constitution establishes amendment principles and adoption requirements, Governance may receive, classify, review, document, and defer constitutional proposals but may not declare an amendment adopted solely under this document.

# Contributor Governance

Every contribution shall remain subject to `CONTRIBUTION_STANDARDS.md`, the applicable canonical documents, and the review level required by the nature of the change.

Contributors may propose and prepare work, but contribution does not confer approval authority. Human authorship, AI assistance, subject-matter expertise, repository access, or prior contributions do not by themselves grant governance authority.

Contributor work shall preserve approved scope. Material discoveries outside that scope should be documented and classified for separate consideration rather than silently included.

A rejected proposal may be revised and resubmitted when its identified defects can be addressed. A deferred proposal should identify the missing evidence, authority, dependency, or project condition required before reconsideration.

Governance decisions concerning contributors shall address the work and applicable responsibilities. They shall not claim ecclesial judgment or spiritual authority over a person.

---

# Review and Approval Governance

## Independence

Independent review requires meaningful separation from authorship. An Independent Reviewer should not review work for which they were the primary author or final decision-maker.

When the project's size prevents full separation of people, the Project Steward shall preserve role separation, disclose the limitation, and seek the strongest reasonably available independent evaluation.

## Conflicts of Interest

Reviewers and approvers shall disclose material personal, financial, organizational, authorship, or other interests that could reasonably affect independent judgment.

A material conflict should be resolved through reassignment, additional review, or explicit documented mitigation.

## Review Findings

Review findings should distinguish:

* required corrections,
* material concerns,
* optional improvements,
* questions requiring clarification,
* and matters outside the approved scope.

## Approval

Approval shall be explicit. Silence, participation, repository access, review completion, or lack of objection does not constitute canonical or release approval.

The approver shall confirm that the correct review level was used, material findings were resolved or dispositioned, validation succeeded, and the approved state is identifiable.

## Disagreement

Unresolved disagreement shall be documented in proportion to its significance.

Disagreement involving constitutional authority, material theology, missing required review, or authority outside Governance shall prevent ordinary approval until properly escalated.

Other disagreements may be resolved by the applicable delegated approver when the decision, reasoning, alternatives, and remaining concerns are transparently recorded.

---

# Decision Records and Transparency

Significant governance decisions shall be recorded in `DECISION_LOG.md` when they:

* establish or materially change delegated authority,
* approve an important governance policy,
* resolve a significant document-boundary question,
* reject an important alternative,
* materially affect project direction or continuity,
* authorize an exceptional or emergency action,
* or provide necessary context for future stewardship.

Routine administration and minor editorial approvals do not require separate decision-log entries unless their cumulative effect establishes a new project practice.

The Project Steward is responsible for ensuring that required decision records are completed. The person making or approving a decision is responsible for the accuracy of its recorded reasoning and disposition.

Undocumented repeated practice shall not silently acquire canonical authority.

---

# Release Governance

A release is an authorized distribution or designation of a verified project state.

Before release approval, the Release Approver shall confirm that:

* the release contains only the intended changes,
* included canonical changes received canonical approval,
* applicable review and validation are complete,
* repository checks are satisfactory,
* known material issues are disclosed,
* and relevant licensing and intellectual-property requirements have been considered.

Documentation releases remain subject to the same principles of scope, review, approval, and verification as other project changes.

Future architecture or implementation releases may require additional technical, security, privacy, or operational validation defined in the canonical documents responsible for those areas.

Initially, the Project Steward serves as Release Approver. Future delegation shall identify the recipient, scope, limitations, effective date, and revocation conditions of the delegated authority.

Release approval does not alter the canonical hierarchy and shall not be used to make unapproved material canonical.

# Conflict, Correction, and Escalation

## Conflict with Higher Authority

When a governance decision or subordinate document conflicts with Holy Scripture or the Constitution, the higher authority shall prevail. The conflict shall be documented, reliance upon the conflicting decision shall cease, and correction shall begin through the appropriate review process.

## Document-Boundary Conflict

When material is placed in the wrong canonical document, it shall be reclassified before approval. Governance shall not retain material merely because drafting has already occurred.

## Noncompliant Contributions

A contribution that fails applicable standards shall be revised, deferred, or rejected. Approval authority may not cure a substantive constitutional or classification defect by declaration alone.

## Unauthorized Changes

Unauthorized changes shall be identified, isolated, and reviewed. They shall not become accepted precedent through the passage of time or continued use.

Correction should preserve unrelated approved work whenever reasonably possible and shall maintain a transparent repository record.

## Governance Disagreement

Governance disagreement should first be addressed through clarification of authority, classification, evidence, and applicable standards.

If disagreement remains material, it shall be escalated to the Project Steward or the properly delegated approver. Constitutional and theological questions shall be escalated to the appropriate constitutional review rather than decided through ordinary governance preference.

## Authority Uncertainty

When no role clearly possesses authority to decide a matter, the matter shall be deferred. Authority shall be clarified through an explicit governance or constitutional action before the decision proceeds.

# Continuity and Succession

Governance shall preserve Divine Ecosystem beyond the memory, availability, or tenure of any individual contributor or steward.

Continuity requires:

* current canonical documents,
* accessible decision records,
* clear role and delegation records,
* reproducible repository history,
* documented unresolved matters,
* and preservation of legal and intellectual-property information.

The transfer of a governance role shall identify:

* the role being transferred,
* the authority included,
* applicable limitations,
* the effective date,
* outstanding decisions or risks,
* and any repository or operational access requiring responsible transfer.

Succession does not permit the transfer of spiritual authority or authority beyond that delegated to the role.

When a steward is temporarily unavailable, routine work may continue only within existing delegated authority. Matters requiring unavailable approval shall be deferred unless a documented delegation or continuity provision authorizes another role to act.

No undocumented conversation, private memory, or AI chat history shall serve as the sole permanent record of a governance decision.

# Governance Review and Amendment

This Governance document may mature as the project grows, provided every change remains consistent with Holy Scripture, the Constitution, and the Foundational Principles.

A proposed governance amendment shall:

* identify the governance problem being addressed,
* remain within Governance's document responsibility,
* disclose changes to roles or delegated authority,
* receive the applicable canonical-document review,
* document significant reasoning and alternatives,
* and receive canonical approval before taking effect.

A governance amendment may not be used as a substitute for constitutional amendment, architectural definition, workflow revision, or backlog planning.

Delegated authority may be revised or revoked through an approved governance decision. Such action shall preserve a clear record of the former authority, the reason for change, and the effective date.

---

# Governance Limitations

Governance may never:

* supersede Holy Scripture,
* supersede the Divine Ecosystem Constitution,
* create or redefine theology,
* alter Divine Core reasoning,
* override constitutional authority,
* bypass required review,
* grant authority beyond that delegated by the Constitution,
* redefine the responsibilities of Architecture, Project Workflow, or any other canonical document,
* treat project approval as spiritual or ecclesial authority,
* make a constitutional amendment through ordinary governance action,
* make architectural implementation canonical before Architecture is established,
* or permit urgency, convenience, consensus, technology, or institutional practice to enlarge governance authority.

Any governance action that exceeds these limitations is outside the authority of this document and shall not govern Divine Ecosystem.

---

# Governance Decision Test

Before adopting a governance decision, ask:

* Does this remain subordinate to Holy Scripture and the Constitution?
* Does this belong in Governance rather than the Constitution, Architecture, Project Workflow, or another canonical document?
* Does it strengthen long-term stewardship without expanding Governance beyond its delegated authority?
* Does it preserve transparency, accountability, and independent review?

If a proposed decision does not satisfy these questions, it shall be revised, reclassified, escalated, or rejected before adoption.

---

# Closing Principle

Governance exists to steward Divine Ecosystem faithfully, transparently, and responsibly under Holy Scripture and the Constitution.

Its purpose is not to create authority, but to exercise delegated responsibility within clear limits; not to replace theological discernment, but to protect the conditions under which faithful work may proceed; and not to control the future through institutional power, but to preserve constitutional integrity, accountable decisions, and responsible continuity for every future stage of the project.
