# Divine Ecosystem Decision Log

## Purpose

This document preserves the reasoning behind significant architectural, constitutional, governance, and philosophical decisions made during the development of Divine Ecosystem.

Its purpose is not to duplicate the Constitution, Architecture, or Governance documents, but to provide historical context explaining why important decisions were made.

This log serves future contributors by preserving institutional knowledge that might otherwise be lost.

---

# Recording Principles

Major decisions should be recorded when they:

* establish a new long-term principle,
* significantly alter project direction,
* reject an important alternative,
* clarify document boundaries,
* or materially influence future development.

Routine implementation details should not be recorded.

---

# Decision Format

Every decision should include:

## Decision

A concise statement describing the decision.

---

## Reasoning

Why the decision was made.

Include the theological, constitutional, architectural, or project reasoning that led to the decision.

---

## Alternatives Considered

Record significant alternatives that were discussed and explain why they were not adopted.

---

## Impact

Describe how the decision affects:

* the Constitution,
* Governance,
* Architecture,
* Workflow,
* or future development.

---

# Initial Decisions

## 2026 — Constitution Governs Principles

### Decision

The Constitution governs enduring theological and constitutional principles rather than architectural implementation.

### Reasoning

Architectural implementation is expected to mature over time.

Constitutional principles should remain stable regardless of future technological or architectural improvements.

### Impact

Architecture becomes free to mature without requiring constitutional amendments.

---

## 2026 — Scripture Remains Supreme

### Decision

Holy Scripture remains the supreme authority over every canonical document, including the Constitution.

### Reasoning

The Constitution derives its authority from its continued faithfulness to God's revealed Word rather than possessing independent authority.

### Impact

Future constitutional review remains accountable to Holy Scripture.

---

## 2026 — Question Determines Evidence

### Decision

The theological question determines what evidence is materially relevant.

### Reasoning

Evidence should be gathered according to its material relevance rather than by predetermined categories.

### Impact

This principle shapes theological reasoning throughout Divine Ecosystem.

---

## Closing Principle

Future contributors should be able to understand not only what decisions were made, but why they were made.

Preserving reasoning strengthens continuity, reduces repeated debate, and encourages responsible long-term stewardship.
