# Divine Ecosystem Canonical Document Hierarchy

## Purpose

This document defines the purpose, scope, and relationship of every canonical document within the Divine Ecosystem.

Its purpose is to preserve a clear separation between enduring constitutional principles, architectural implementation, project governance, and development workflow.

No canonical document should duplicate the responsibility of another. Each document exists to answer a distinct class of questions while remaining accountable to Holy Scripture and the Divine Ecosystem Constitution.

---

# Canonical Hierarchy

The canonical documents of Divine Ecosystem exist in the following conceptual order.

## 1. Holy Scripture

Holy Scripture is the supreme authority for every theological principle contained within Divine Ecosystem.

Every canonical document remains accountable to the authority of God's revealed Word.

---

## 2. Constitution

The Constitution establishes the enduring theological and constitutional principles of Divine Ecosystem.

It answers permanent questions regarding theology, authority, hermeneutics, and constitutional order.

The Constitution governs enduring principles rather than implementation.

---

## 3. Foundational Principles

Foundational Principles describe the enduring project philosophy and design principles of Divine Ecosystem.

They operate in submission to Holy Scripture and the Constitution.

---

## 4. Governance

Governance defines how the project is stewarded.

It establishes policies for constitutional amendments, project decision-making, document approval, reviews, releases, and long-term stewardship.

Governance shall always remain consistent with both Holy Scripture and the Constitution.

---

## 5. Architecture

Architecture defines how Divine Ecosystem fulfills the constitutional principles established by the Constitution.

Architecture may evolve as understanding, technology, and implementation mature.

Architectural changes shall never violate the enduring principles established by the Constitution.

---

## 6. Project Workflow

Project Workflow defines the process used to develop and maintain the canonical documents of Divine Ecosystem.

It establishes the standard workflow for discovery, drafting, review, revision, verification, and canonical approval.

---

## 7. Roadmap

The Roadmap defines the planned direction and future development of Divine Ecosystem.

Unlike the Constitution, Architecture, and Governance, the Roadmap is expected to evolve regularly as priorities mature.

---

## 8. Backlog

The Backlog records proposed work that has not yet been completed.

It serves as the project's working task list and planning document.

---

# Guiding Principle

Every canonical document exists for a distinct purpose.

Questions should always be placed into the document whose purpose most naturally answers them.

When uncertainty exists, enduring principles belong in the Constitution, implementation belongs in Architecture, stewardship belongs in Governance, workflow belongs in Project Workflow, planning belongs in the Roadmap, and unfinished work belongs in the Backlog.

This separation preserves both constitutional stability and architectural flexibility throughout the continued development of Divine Ecosystem.
