# Divine Ecosystem Contribution Standards

## Purpose

This document establishes the standards by which every contribution to Divine Ecosystem shall be evaluated before becoming part of the canonical project.

Its purpose is to preserve theological faithfulness, constitutional consistency, architectural integrity, and long-term maintainability throughout the continued development of Divine Ecosystem.

These standards apply equally to human contributors and AI-assisted development.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Every contribution should strengthen Divine Ecosystem.

No contribution should weaken an existing constitutional principle, reduce transparency, introduce unnecessary complexity, or compromise theological integrity.

---

# Constitutional Standards

Every proposed contribution shall be evaluated according to the following questions.

### Constitutional Consistency

Does the contribution remain consistent with:

* Holy Scripture?
* The Divine Ecosystem Constitution?
* The established constitutional philosophy?

If not, it shall not be accepted.

---

### Architectural Consistency

Does the contribution belong in Architecture rather than the Constitution?

Does it preserve the established separation between enduring principles and implementation?

---

### Theological Consistency

Does the contribution:

* faithfully represent Scripture?
* distinguish evidence from inference?
* distinguish interpretation from doctrine?
* acknowledge uncertainty where appropriate?

---

### Transparency

Does the contribution improve transparency?

Reasoning should become more understandable rather than less understandable.

Hidden assumptions should be reduced whenever reasonably possible.

---

### Simplicity

Does the contribution simplify the project without sacrificing accuracy?

Complexity should only be introduced when it materially improves faithful theological understanding.

---

### Reusability

Does the contribution strengthen the shared foundation rather than a single implementation?

Whenever practical:

Build once.

Benefit everywhere.

---

### Future Maintainability

Will this contribution remain understandable years from now?

Would future contributors naturally understand why it exists?

---

# Review Expectations

Major contributions should undergo:

* independent review,
* architectural evaluation,
* repository verification,
* and canonical approval

before becoming part of the project.

---

# Closing Principle

Every contribution should leave Divine Ecosystem stronger than it was before.

Faithfulness, transparency, clarity, and constitutional consistency shall always take precedence over novelty.
