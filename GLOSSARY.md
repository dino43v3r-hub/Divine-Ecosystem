# Divine Ecosystem Glossary

## Purpose

This glossary establishes the canonical definitions of key terms used throughout the Divine Ecosystem.

Its purpose is to preserve consistent terminology across the Constitution, Governance, Architecture, Workflow, and all future canonical documents.

Definitions within this glossary should remain consistent with Holy Scripture and the Constitution.

---

# Canonical Terms

## Divine Ecosystem

The complete theological research environment governed by the Constitution and composed of its canonical documents, architecture, shared reasoning foundation, and future applications.

---

## Constitution

The canonical document establishing the enduring theological and constitutional principles of Divine Ecosystem.

---

## Holy Scripture

The canonical Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments, recognized as the inspired Word of God and the supreme authority for all theological study within Divine Ecosystem.

---

## Canonical Document

A document officially recognized as part of the governing framework of Divine Ecosystem.

Canonical documents establish enduring principles, stewardship, implementation, planning, or project workflow according to their defined purpose.

---

## Constitutional Principle

An enduring theological or constitutional truth that should remain stable regardless of changes in architecture, implementation, or technology.

---

## Governing Principle

A principle that directs how Divine Ecosystem is developed, evaluated, or maintained.

Not every governing principle is constitutional.

---

## Architecture

The implementation by which Divine Ecosystem fulfills the constitutional principles established in the Constitution.

---

## Governance

The stewardship of Divine Ecosystem, including project oversight, constitutional review, contribution standards, and long-term maintenance.

---

## Project Workflow

The defined process used to discover, draft, review, approve, and maintain canonical documents.

---

## Materially Relevant Evidence

Evidence that directly bears upon the theological question under consideration.

Material relevance is determined by the theological question rather than by predetermined categories.

---

## Faithful Theological Understanding

The most responsible theological understanding that can be reached through transparent, biblically accountable reasoning under the supreme authority of Holy Scripture.

---

# Living Vocabulary

This glossary may expand as Divine Ecosystem matures.

New definitions should preserve consistency with existing canonical terminology and should avoid redefining established theological or constitutional concepts.

---

# Closing Principle

Shared language strengthens shared understanding.

Maintaining clear and consistent terminology protects the long-term coherence of Divine Ecosystem.
