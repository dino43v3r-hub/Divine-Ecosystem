# Divine Ecosystem Project Workflow

## Purpose

This document defines the standard development workflow for the canonical documents of Divine Ecosystem.

Its purpose is to preserve a disciplined process that prioritizes theological faithfulness, constitutional stability, architectural flexibility, and transparent peer review.

This workflow governs how enduring project documents are developed. It does not govern the theological content of those documents, which remains under the authority of Holy Scripture and the Divine Ecosystem Constitution.

---

# Guiding Principles

The development of Divine Ecosystem shall follow these principles:

* Scripture first.
* Reasoning before voice.
* Foundation before application.
* Build once, benefit everywhere.
* Constitution governs enduring principles.
* Architecture governs implementation.
* Governance governs stewardship.
* Applications fulfill the Constitution through the Architecture.

---

# Canonical Development Workflow

Every canonical document shall be developed using the following process.

## Phase 1 — Discover the Question

Before writing begins, identify the enduring question the document or section must answer.

No drafting shall begin until the governing question is understood.

---

## Phase 2 — Discover the Governing Principle

Identify the enduring principle that answers the governing question.

The goal is to discover the principle rather than invent it.

---

## Phase 3 — Separate Principle from Implementation

Determine whether the discovered principle belongs in:

* the Constitution,
* Architecture,
* Governance,
* or another canonical document.

Enduring principles belong in the Constitution.

Implementation belongs in Architecture.

Project stewardship belongs in Governance.

---

## Phase 4 — Draft

Draft the document while preserving the established voice, style, and constitutional philosophy of Divine Ecosystem.

---

## Phase 5 — Repository Review

Commit the draft to the canonical repository.

---

## Phase 6 — Independent Review

Conduct an independent review (such as Codex) without permitting unauthorized rewriting.

The reviewer shall evaluate consistency, clarity, and constitutional integrity.

---

## Phase 7 — Architectural Evaluation

Evaluate every recommendation.

Recommendations are accepted or rejected through deliberate architectural reasoning rather than automatically applied.

---

## Phase 8 — Approved Revisions

Implement only those revisions that have been explicitly approved.

---

## Phase 9 — Verification

Verify the repository after every revision to ensure only the approved changes were made.

---

## Phase 10 — Canonical Lock

Once verified, the document or section becomes the current canonical version until future constitutional review determines that an enduring principle requires amendment.

---

# Constitutional Drafting Tests

Every proposed constitutional addition should be evaluated using the following questions.

* Does it answer an enduring constitutional question?
* Does it establish an enduring principle?
* Would it remain true if the architecture changed?
* Would it remain true if every application changed?
* Would removing it eliminate a unique constitutional principle?
* Does it remain accountable to Holy Scripture?

If the answer to these questions is no, the content likely belongs outside the Constitution.

---

# Continuous Improvement

The workflow itself may mature through experience.

However, any changes shall preserve the principles of transparent reasoning, constitutional stability, faithful theological study, and accountability to Holy Scripture.
